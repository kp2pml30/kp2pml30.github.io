Giving & Receiving — あげる・もらう・くれる

Japanese has different verbs for "give" and "receive" depending on the direction (who gives to whom) and the speaker's perspective.

Basic verbs

VerbDirectionMeaningKey rule
あげるI/someone → someone elseTo give (away from speaker)The giver is the subject. Never used when someone gives to the speaker.
くれるSomeone → me/my groupTo give (toward speaker)The giver is the subject. Used when the speaker (or someone close) receives.
もらうI/someone ← someone elseTo receiveThe receiver is the subject. The giver is marked with に or から.

Examples

JapaneseTranslationWhy this verb
私は友達にプレゼントをあげたわたしはともだちにプレゼントをあげたI gave my friend a present.I → friend (away from speaker).
友達が私にプレゼントをくれたMy friend gave me a present.Friend → me (toward speaker).
私は友達にプレゼントをもらったI received a present from my friend.I am the receiver (subject).
田中さんは山田さんにお菓子をあげたたなかさんはやまださんにおかしをあげたTanaka gave Yamada sweets.Third person → third person (neutral).
田中さんが弟にお菓子をくれたたなかさんがおとうとにおかしをくれたTanaka gave my brother sweets.Third person → my family member (toward speaker's group).

て form — doing favors

Combine て form with あげる/くれる/もらう to express doing (or having done) something as a favor.

PatternMeaningExample
～てあげるI do something for someone (as a favor).荷物を持ってあげたにもつをもってあげた— I carried the bags for them.
～てくれるSomeone does something for me (I'm grateful).友達が教えてくれたともだちがおしえてくれた— My friend taught me (and I appreciate it).
～てもらうI have/get someone to do something for me.先生に説明してもらったせんせいにせつめいしてもらった— I had the teacher explain it to me.

Nuance differences

PatternFocusNuance
～てあげるOn the doer (I/someone)Can sound condescending if used about yourself too directly. ～てあげましょうか is safer for offering.
～てくれるOn the doer (someone else)Emphasizes gratitude — "they kindly did X for me". Very natural in Japanese.
～てもらうOn the receiver (I/someone)Emphasizes that you arranged or benefited from the action. ～てもらえますか is a polite request.

Making requests with giving/receiving

ExpressionPolitenessExample
～てくれる？Casual手伝ってくれるてつだってくれる？— Can you help me?
～てくれない？Casual (softer)ちょっと待ってくれない？ — Could you wait a sec?
～てもらえる？Casual-polite写真を撮ってもらえるしゃしんをとってもらえる？— Could you take a photo?
～てもらえますかPolite説明してもらえますか — Could you explain?
～ていただけますかVery polite確認していただけますかかくにんしていただけますか— Could you please confirm?
～ていただけないでしょうかMost politeお時間をいただけないでしょうか — Might I have some of your time?

Set phrases from giving/receiving

PhraseLiteral originMeaning & usage
いただきますいただく (humble "to receive") in ます formSaid before eating — "I humbly receive (this food)". Expresses gratitude to the cook, the ingredients, etc.
ごちそうさまでしたご馳走ごちそう= feast + さま + でしたSaid after eating — "It was a feast / Thank you for the meal".
～をいただけますかいただく potential + ます + かCould I have ~? Polite way to order or request something. お水をいただけますか = Could I have water?
～をくださいくださる (honorific "to give") imperativePlease give me ~. Standard polite request. これをください = I'll have this, please.
お待たせしましたおまたせしました待たせる (to make wait) in humble formSorry to have kept you waiting. Said by staff, waiters, etc.
いらっしゃいませいらっしゃる (honorific "to come/be") in ます imperativeWelcome! Said by shop/restaurant staff to customers.
