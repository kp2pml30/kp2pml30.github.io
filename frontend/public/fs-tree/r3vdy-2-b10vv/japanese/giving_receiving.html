Giving & Receiving — あげる・もらう・くれる

Japanese has different verbs for "give" and "receive" depending on the direction (who gives to whom) and the speaker's perspective.

Basic verbs

Verb Direction Meaning Key rule
あげる I/someone → someone else To give (away from speaker) The giver is the subject. Never used when someone gives to the speaker.
くれる Someone → me/my group To give (toward speaker) The giver is the subject. Used when the speaker (or someone close) receives.
もらう I/someone ← someone else To receive The receiver is the subject. The giver is marked with に or から.

Examples

Japanese Translation Why this verb
私は友達にプレゼントをあげた（わたしはともだちにプレゼントをあげた） I gave my friend a present. I → friend (away from speaker).
友達が私にプレゼントをくれた My friend gave me a present. Friend → me (toward speaker).
私は友達にプレゼントをもらった I received a present from my friend. I am the receiver (subject).
田中さんは山田さんにお菓子をあげた（たなかさんはやまださんにおかしをあげた） Tanaka gave Yamada sweets. Third person → third person (neutral).
田中さんが弟にお菓子をくれた（たなかさんがおとうとにおかしをくれた） Tanaka gave my brother sweets. Third person → my family member (toward speaker's group).

て form — doing favors

Combine て form with あげる/くれる/もらう to express doing (or having done) something as a favor.

Pattern Meaning Example
～てあげる I do something for someone (as a favor). 荷物を持ってあげた（にもつをもってあげた）— I carried the bags for them.
～てくれる Someone does something for me (I'm grateful). 友達が教えてくれた（ともだちがおしえてくれた）— My friend taught me (and I appreciate it).
～てもらう I have/get someone to do something for me. 先生に説明してもらった（せんせいにせつめいしてもらった）— I had the teacher explain it to me.

Nuance differences

Pattern Focus Nuance
～てあげる On the doer (I/someone) Can sound condescending if used about yourself too directly. ～てあげましょうか is safer for offering.
～てくれる On the doer (someone else) Emphasizes gratitude — "they kindly did X for me". Very natural in Japanese.
～てもらう On the receiver (I/someone) Emphasizes that you arranged or benefited from the action. ～てもらえますか is a polite request.

Keigo versions

Plain Respectful (尊敬語) Humble (謙譲語)
あげる — 差し上げる（さしあげる）
くれる くださる —
もらう — いただく
Plain Keigo Example
～てあげる ～て差し上げる（てさしあげる） お持ちして差し上げます — I'll carry it for you (very humble).
～てくれる ～てくださる 先生が教えてくださった — The teacher kindly taught me.
～てもらう ～ていただく 見ていただけますか（みていただけますか）— Could you take a look? (polite request)

Making requests with giving/receiving

Expression Politeness Example
～てくれる？ Casual 手伝ってくれる？（てつだってくれる）— Can you help me?
～てくれない？ Casual (softer) ちょっと待ってくれない？ — Could you wait a sec?
～てもらえる？ Casual-polite 写真を撮ってもらえる？（しゃしんをとってもらえる）— Could you take a photo?
～てもらえますか Polite 説明してもらえますか — Could you explain?
～ていただけますか Very polite 確認していただけますか（かくにんしていただけますか）— Could you please confirm?
～ていただけないでしょうか Most polite お時間をいただけないでしょうか — Might I have some of your time?

Set phrases from giving/receiving

Phrase Literal origin Meaning & usage
いただきます いただく (humble "to receive") in ます form Said before eating — "I humbly receive (this food)". Expresses gratitude to the cook, the ingredients, etc.
ごちそうさまでした ご馳走（ごちそう）= feast + さま + でした Said after eating — "It was a feast / Thank you for the meal".
～をいただけますか いただく potential + ます + か Could I have ~? Polite way to order or request something. お水をいただけますか = Could I have water?
～をください くださる (honorific "to give") imperative Please give me ~. Standard polite request. これをください = I'll have this, please.
お待たせしました（おまたせしました） 待たせる (to make wait) in humble form Sorry to have kept you waiting. Said by staff, waiters, etc.
いらっしゃいませ いらっしゃる (honorific "to come/be") in ます imperative Welcome! Said by shop/restaurant staff to customers.
