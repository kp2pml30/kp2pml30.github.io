は vs が

The most notorious particle distinction in Japanese.

は (topic)が (subject)
Marks the topic — "as for X, ..."Marks the subject — identifies who/what does the action
Known/old informationNew information / answer to "who/what?"
Contrast: コーヒーは飲む（けど紅茶は飲まない）— Coffee, I drink (but not tea)Identification: 誰が来た？田中さんが来た — Who came? Tanaka came.
General statements: 猫はかわいい — Cats are cute (in general)Specific events: 猫がいる — There's a cat (right there)
Main clause topicSubordinate clause subject: 私が作った料理 — The food that I made

に vs で

にで
Location of existence (いる/ある): 部屋にいる — I'm in the roomLocation of action: 部屋で勉強する — I study in the room
Destination: 学校に行く — Go to schoolMeans/method: バスで行く — Go by bus
Time point: 3時に会う — Meet at 3Scope/range: 3日で終わるおわる— Finish in 3 days
Recipient: 先生に聞く — Ask the teacherCause: 台風で休みたいふうでやすみ— Off because of typhoon
Purpose: 買い物に行くかいものにいく— Go shoppingMaterial: 木で作るきでつくる— Made of wood

へ vs に (direction)

へ（え）に
Emphasizes direction/journey — "toward"Emphasizes destination/arrival — "to"
日本へ行く — Head toward Japan日本に行く — Go to Japan (arrive there)
Often interchangeable in modern speechRequired with specific arrival: 駅に着くえきにつく— Arrive at the station (not へ)

Limiting particles — だけ vs しか vs ばかり

ParticleVerb formNuanceExample
だけAffirmativeOnly — neutral, just a fact.水だけ飲んだみずだけのんだ— I only drank water.
しかNegative (always)Only — implies "not enough" or "unfortunately only".水しか飲まなかった — I drank nothing but water (and I wish there was more).
ばかりAffirmativeNothing but — often negative connotation, excess.ゲームばかりしている — You do nothing but play games.

Listing particles — と vs や vs とか vs など

ParticleList typeExample
とComplete list (A and B, that's all)犬と猫いぬとねこ— Dogs and cats (specifically these two)
やIncomplete list (A and B, among others)犬や猫 — Dogs and cats (and other animals)
とかCasual incomplete list — "like A and B"寿司とかラーメンとかすしとかラーメンとか— Like sushi, ramen, etc.
などEtc. / and so on (written/formal)野菜や果物などやさいやくだものなど— Vegetables, fruits, etc.
