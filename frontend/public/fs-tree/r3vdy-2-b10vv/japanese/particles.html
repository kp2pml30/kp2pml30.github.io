Particles

Grammar Cases — Cross-Language Comparison

Japanese uses particles (postpositions) instead of word endings to mark grammatical roles. Here's how they map to cases in other languages.

は vs が

The most notorious particle distinction in Japanese.

は (topic) が (subject)
Marks the topic — "as for X, ..." Marks the subject — identifies who/what does the action
Known/old information New information / answer to "who/what?"
Contrast: コーヒーは飲む（けど紅茶は飲まない）— Coffee, I drink (but not tea) Identification: 誰が来た？田中さんが来た — Who came? Tanaka came.
General statements: 猫はかわいい — Cats are cute (in general) Specific events: 猫がいる — There's a cat (right there)
Main clause topic Subordinate clause subject: 私が作った料理 — The food that I made

に vs で

に で
Location of existence (いる/ある): 部屋にいる — I'm in the room Location of action: 部屋で勉強する — I study in the room
Destination: 学校に行く — Go to school Means/method: バスで行く — Go by bus
Time point: 3時に会う — Meet at 3 Scope/range: 3日で終わる（おわる）— Finish in 3 days
Recipient: 先生に聞く — Ask the teacher Cause: 台風で休み（たいふうでやすみ）— Off because of typhoon
Purpose: 買い物に行く（かいものにいく）— Go shopping Material: 木で作る（きでつくる）— Made of wood

へ vs に (direction)

へ（え） に
Emphasizes direction/journey — "toward" Emphasizes destination/arrival — "to"
日本へ行く — Head toward Japan 日本に行く — Go to Japan (arrive there)
Often interchangeable in modern speech Required with specific arrival: 駅に着く（えきにつく）— Arrive at the station (not へ)

Limiting particles — だけ vs しか vs ばかり

Particle Verb form Nuance Example
だけ Affirmative Only — neutral, just a fact. 水だけ飲んだ（みずだけのんだ）— I only drank water.
しか Negative (always) Only — implies "not enough" or "unfortunately only". 水しか飲まなかった — I drank nothing but water (and I wish there was more).
ばかり Affirmative Nothing but — often negative connotation, excess. ゲームばかりしている — You do nothing but play games.

Listing particles — と vs や vs とか vs など

Particle List type Example
と Complete list (A and B, that's all) 犬と猫（いぬとねこ）— Dogs and cats (specifically these two)
や Incomplete list (A and B, among others) 犬や猫 — Dogs and cats (and other animals)
とか Casual incomplete list — "like A and B" 寿司とかラーメンとか（すしとかラーメンとか）— Like sushi, ramen, etc.
など Etc. / and so on (written/formal) 野菜や果物など（やさいやくだものなど）— Vegetables, fruits, etc.

Other important particles

Particle Function Example
も Also / too / even 私も行く（わたしもいく）— I'll go too.
一つも食べなかった（ひとつもたべなかった）— Didn't eat even one.
か Question / or 行きますか — Will you go?
コーヒーか紅茶（こうちゃ）— Coffee or tea.
よ Emphasis / informing (sentence-final) おいしいよ — It's good, you know!
ね Seeking agreement (sentence-final) いい天気ですね（いいてんきですね）— Nice weather, isn't it?
の Explanatory / question (sentence-final, casual) どうしたの — What happened?
まで Until / as far as 5時まで待つ（ごじまでまつ）— Wait until 5.
東京まで — As far as Tokyo.
までに By (deadline) 金曜日までに出す（きんようびまでにだす）— Submit by Friday.
より Than (comparison) 犬より猫が好き（いぬよりねこがすき）— I like cats more than dogs.
ほど To the extent / as much as (often negative) 思ったほど難しくない（おもったほどむずかしくない）— Not as hard as I thought.
