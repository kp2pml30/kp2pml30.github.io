Japanese Positive & Complimentary Language

Useful words and expressions for praising, encouraging, and being polite in Japanese.

Complimenting people

Word Reading Meaning Notes
かっこいい / 格好いいかっこいいCool / handsomeFor people, things, actions. Very common.
かわいい / 可愛いかわいいCute / adorableUsed for people, animals, things. Extremely versatile.
きれい / 綺麗きれいBeautiful / cleanな-adjective. For people, scenery, rooms.
美しいうつくしいBeautiful (formal/poetic)More literary than きれい.
美人びじんBeautiful woman / beautyNoun. 美男（びなん）/ イケメン for men.
素敵すてきLovely / wonderfulな-adjective. For people, outfits, ideas.
優しいやさしいKind / gentleOne of the best compliments for personality.
頭がいいあたまがいいSmart / clever賢い（かしこい）is similar but slightly more bookish.
真面目まじめSerious / diligent / responsiblePositive in Japanese — means hardworking and reliable.
面白いおもしろいInteresting / funnyFor people, stories, ideas.
明るいあかるいBright / cheerful (personality)性格が明るい（せいかくがあかるい）= cheerful personality.
すごい—Amazing / awesomeVery versatile. すげー is the rough casual form.
えらい—Admirable / great (for effort)Used to praise someone for working hard or doing the right thing.

Praising things & situations

Word Reading Meaning Notes
おいしい / 美味しいおいしいDeliciousうまい is casual/masculine equivalent. 激うま（げきうま）= super delicious.
最高さいこうThe best / awesomeVery enthusiastic praise.
素晴らしいすばらしいWonderful / splendidSlightly formal. Strong praise.
完璧かんぺきPerfectな-adjective.
やばい—Amazing / crazy (good)Slang. Originally "dangerous" but now mostly positive among young people.
半端ないはんぱないIncredible / insane (good)マジ半端ない = seriously incredible.
神かみGod-tier / godlySlang. 神ゲー = amazing game, 神曲（かみきょく）= amazing song.
最強さいきょうStrongest / the GOATSlang praise for the best at something.
天才てんさいGeniusUsed as genuine or playful praise.

Encouragement

Expression Meaning Notes
頑張って（がんばって）Do your best! / Good luck!The most common encouragement. 頑張れ（がんばれ）is the imperative form.
応援してる（おうえんしてる）I'm rooting for you!Supportive, warm.
大丈夫（だいじょうぶ）It's okay / You'll be fineReassuring.
気にしないで（きにしないで）Don't worry about it気にすんな is the rough casual form.
ドンマイDon't mind / No worriesFrom English "don't mind". Casual.
よくやったWell done! / Good job!よくできました is the polite/teacher version.
さすがAs expected (of you) / ImpressivePraises someone for living up to their reputation. さすがだね = you're amazing as always.
その調子（そのちょうし）Keep it up! / That's the way!Encouraging someone to continue what they're doing.
無理しないで（むりしないで）Don't push yourself too hardCaring, considerate.

Agreeing & affirming

Expression Meaning Notes
いいねNice! / Sounds good!Casual approval. いいですね is polite.
そうだねThat's right / YeahAgreeing casually.
確かに（たしかに）Indeed / That's trueAcknowledging a good point.
なるほどI see / Makes senseShowing understanding. Slightly casual.
その通り（そのとおり）Exactly / That's correctStrong agreement.
間違いない（まちがいない）No doubt about it / For sureEmphatic agreement.

さしすせそ — The Art of Flattering Responses

A well-known mnemonic for conversational responses that make the other person feel good. Especially popular in dating/social advice.

Expression Meaning When to use
ささすが！As expected of you! / Impressive!When someone does something well or shows competence.
し知らなかった！（しらなかった）I didn't know that!When someone tells you something — makes them feel knowledgeable.
すすごい！Amazing! / Wow!General-purpose impressed reaction.
せセンスいい！You have great taste/sense!Complimenting someone's choices — fashion, food, ideas.
そそうなんだ！Really? / Is that so!Showing genuine interest in what someone is saying.

Expression Meaning Notes

Terms of endearment

Term Usage Notes
Name + ちゃんAffectionate name suffixUsed for close friends, partners, children. ゆきちゃん, あやちゃん.
Name + くんFamiliar suffix (usually for males)Also used by women for boyfriends.
Name without suffix (呼び捨て / よびすて)Using just the first nameShows closeness. Calling someone by first name alone is already intimate in Japanese.
あなたYou / DearWives sometimes call husbands あなた. Otherwise avoid — it can sound condescending.

Cute sentence endings

Ending Effect Example
～だよ / ～だよねSoft assertion / seeking agreementおいしいよね — It's good, right?
～ね / ～ねーSeeking confirmation, friendly toneいい天気だね（いいてんきだね）— Nice weather, huh?
～の / ～なのSoft explanatory (often feminine)ちょっと疲れたの（つかれたの）— I'm a bit tired.
～かなI wonder... (soft/indirect)行こうかな — Maybe I'll go...
～よGentle emphasis / informing大丈夫だよ（だいじょうぶだよ）— It's fine, you know.
～もん / ～ものBecause...! (slightly childish/whiny)だって眠いもん（ねむいもん）— Because I'm sleepy!
