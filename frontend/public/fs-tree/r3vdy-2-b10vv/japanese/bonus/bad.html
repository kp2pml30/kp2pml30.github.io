Japanese Bad Words & Vulgar Language

Disclaimer: This page is for educational purposes only — to help learners recognize and understand these words in context (anime, manga, conversations). Knowing what words mean helps you avoid using them inappropriately.

Japanese swearing is generally milder than in English. Context and tone matter a lot — some of these are playful among friends but offensive in formal settings.

Swear words & exclamations

WordReadingMeaningNotes
くそ / 糞くそShit / damnVery common swear. Also used as prefix: くそ暑いくそあつい= damn hot.
臭いくさいStinky / smells badNot a swear itself, but rude to say to someone. くせー is rougher.
畜生ちくしょうDamn it!Classic exclamation of frustration. Literally "beast".
くたばれ—Drop dead!Very harsh. Imperative of くたばる (to drop dead).
ふざけるな—Don't mess with me! / Cut the crap!ふざけんな is the casual contraction.
うるさい / うるせー—Shut up! / Noisy!黙れだまれis harsher — "shut your mouth".
死ねしねDie!Imperative of 死ぬ. Very harsh insult.
消えろきえろGet lost! / Disappear!Imperative of 消える.

Rude speech patterns

PatternMeaningExample
～やがるExpresses contempt for someone's action — "that bastard did..."あいつ、逃げやがったにげやがった— That bastard ran away.
～くせにEven though (accusatory) — "despite being..."何も知らないくせになにもしらないくせに— Even though you know nothing...
てめえ / てめーYou (very aggressive)てめー、何やってんだ — What the hell are you doing?
おまえ / お前You (rough, male speech)お前が悪いおまえがわるい— It's your fault.
きさま / 貴様You (hostile, archaic-sounding)Mostly in fiction/anime. Extremely rude in real life.
～じゃねーよRough negative — "it's not..."知らねーよしらねーよ— I don't know (and I don't care).
