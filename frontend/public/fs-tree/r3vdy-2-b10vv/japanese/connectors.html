Sentence Connectors

Japanese connects clauses using conjunctive particles and expressions. They attach to the end of the first clause.

Reason / Cause

Joiner Attaches to Nuance Example
から Plain or polite form Because — subjective reason, often justifying your own actions or opinions. 暑い（あつい）から、窓（まど）を開けた（あけた）— It was hot, so I opened the window.
ので Plain form (な-adj/noun + な + ので) Because — softer, more objective. Preferred in polite speech and writing. 雨（あめ）なので、出かけない（でかけない）— Because it's raining, I won't go out.
し Plain form (な-adj/noun + だし) Listing reasons (and besides...). Implies there are more reasons beyond what's said. 安い（やすい）し、おいしいし、よく行く（いく）— It's cheap, and tasty, so I go often.
て form Verb て form / adj くて / noun で Cause/sequence — neutral connection. The first event leads to or causes the second. 風邪をひいて（かぜをひいて）、学校を休んだ（やすんだ）— I caught a cold and missed school.

Contrast / But

Joiner Attaches to Nuance Example
けど / けれど / けれども Plain or polite form But — casual (けど) to formal (けれども). Also used to soften or introduce a topic. 高い（たかい）けど、買った（かった）— It was expensive, but I bought it.
が Polite form (ます/です) But — formal, written. Also used to softly introduce a topic or request. すみませんが、ちょっとお聞きしたいのですが — Excuse me, I'd like to ask something...
のに Plain form (な-adj/noun + な + のに) Even though / despite — implies frustration, disappointment, or surprise. 約束した（やくそくした）のに、来なかった（こなかった）— Even though they promised, they didn't come.
ても / でも て form + も Even if — hypothetical concession. 雨が降っても（ふっても）、行く — Even if it rains, I'll go.
くせに Plain form (な-adj/noun + の + くせに) Even though — critical/accusatory. Implies someone is not acting as expected. 知っている（しっている）くせに、教えない（おしえない）— Even though you know, you won't tell me.

Condition / If

Joiner Attaches to Nuance Example
たら た form + ら If/when — general purpose. Often for one-time or specific situations. Also "when (it happened)". 駅に着いたら（えきについたら）、電話して（でんわして）— When you arrive at the station, call me.
ば Conditional form (えば/ければ) If — general/hypothetical conditions. Focuses on the condition itself. 安ければ（やすければ）、買う — If it's cheap, I'll buy it.
なら Plain form / noun / な-adj stem If (it's the case that) — reacting to something the listener said or a known situation. 日本に行く（にほんにいく）なら、京都がいい（きょうとがいい）— If you're going to Japan, Kyoto is good.
と Plain present form When/if — automatic, habitual, or natural results. Cannot be used with volition/requests. 春になると（はるになると）、桜が咲く（さくらがさく）— When spring comes, the cherry blossoms bloom.
とき（に）/ 時（に） Plain form + とき (な-adj + な + とき, noun + の + とき) When — a time/occasion when something happens. Dictionary form = action not yet done; た form = action already done. 日本に行った（にほんにいった）とき、お寺を見た（おてらをみた）— When I went to Japan, I saw temples.
日本に行くとき、パスポートがいる — When (before) going to Japan, you need a passport.

Sequence / And / Then

Joiner Attaches to Nuance Example
て form Verb て form And then — sequential actions or simultaneous states. 起きて（おきて）、顔を洗って（かおをあらって）、朝ごはんを食べた — I woke up, washed my face, and ate breakfast.
てから て form + から After doing — emphasizes that the first action is completed before the second starts. 宿題をしてから（しゅくだいをしてから）、遊ぶ（あそぶ）— After doing homework, I'll play.
たあとで / あとで た form + あとで After — the second action happens some time after the first. 食べたあとで、散歩した（さんぽした）— After eating, I took a walk.
前に（まえに） 辞書形 + 前に Before doing something. 寝る前に（ねるまえに）、本を読む（ほんをよむ）— Before sleeping, I read a book.
ながら ます stem + ながら While doing — two simultaneous actions. The main action is the second clause. 音楽を聞きながら（おんがくをききながら）、勉強する（べんきょうする）— I study while listening to music.
たり～たりする た form + り Doing things like ... and ... — non-exhaustive list of actions. 週末は映画を見たり（えいがをみたり）、買い物したり（かいものしたり）する — On weekends I watch movies, go shopping, etc.

Purpose / In order to

Joiner Attaches to Nuance Example
ために 辞書形 / noun + の + ために In order to — purpose (intentional). Also "because of" with た form. 日本語を勉強するために（にほんごをべんきょうするために）、日本に来た — I came to Japan to study Japanese.
ように 辞書形 / ない form + ように So that — goal/hope, often for things not fully in your control. 忘れない（わすれない）ように、メモした — I took notes so I wouldn't forget.
のに 辞書形 / noun + に For the purpose of — what something is used for. この道具は料理を作る（りょうりをつくる）のに使う（つかう）— This tool is used for cooking.

Addition / Moreover

Joiner Attaches to Nuance Example
し Plain form And also / besides — listing multiple reasons or qualities. 頭もいい（あたまもいい）し、優しい（やさしい）し — They're smart, and kind too.
それに Sentence-initial Moreover / on top of that — adds supporting information. 安いし、それに、おいしい — It's cheap, and on top of that, it's delicious.
その上（そのうえ） Sentence-initial Furthermore — more formal than それに. 経験がある（けいけんがある）。その上、資格も持っている（しかくももっている）— They have experience. Furthermore, they have qualifications.
上に（うえに） Plain form + 上に (な-adj + な + 上に) In addition to — adds to what was already said, often compounding. 雨が降った上に（ふったうえに）、風も強かった（かぜもつよかった）— On top of the rain, the wind was strong too.
