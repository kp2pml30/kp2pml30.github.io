Sentence Connectors

Japanese connects clauses using conjunctive particles and expressions. They attach to the end of the first clause.

Reason / Cause

JoinerAttaches toNuanceExample
からPlain or polite formBecause — subjective reason, often justifying your own actions or opinions.暑いあついから、窓まどを開けたあけた— It was hot, so I opened the window.
のでPlain form (な-adj/noun + な + ので)Because — softer, more objective. Preferred in polite speech and writing.雨あめなので、出かけないでかけない— Because it's raining, I won't go out.
しPlain form (な-adj/noun + だし)Listing reasons (and besides...). Implies there are more reasons beyond what's said.安いやすいし、おいしいし、よく行くいく— It's cheap, and tasty, so I go often.
て formVerb て form / adj くて / noun でCause/sequence — neutral connection. The first event leads to or causes the second.風邪をひいてかぜをひいて、学校を休んだやすんだ— I caught a cold and missed school.

Contrast / But

JoinerAttaches toNuanceExample
けど / けれど / けれどもPlain or polite formBut — casual (けど) to formal (けれども). Also used to soften or introduce a topic.高いたかいけど、買ったかった— It was expensive, but I bought it.
がPolite form (ます/です)But — formal, written. Also used to softly introduce a topic or request.すみませんが、ちょっとお聞きしたいのですが — Excuse me, I'd like to ask something...
のにPlain form (な-adj/noun + な + のに)Even though / despite — implies frustration, disappointment, or surprise.約束したやくそくしたのに、来なかったこなかった— Even though they promised, they didn't come.
ても / でもて form + もEven if — hypothetical concession.雨が降ってもふっても、行く — Even if it rains, I'll go.
くせにPlain form (な-adj/noun + の + くせに)Even though — critical/accusatory. Implies someone is not acting as expected.知っているしっているくせに、教えないおしえない— Even though you know, you won't tell me.

Condition / If

JoinerAttaches toNuanceExample
たらた form + らIf/when — general purpose. Often for one-time or specific situations. Also "when (it happened)".駅に着いたらえきについたら、電話してでんわして— When you arrive at the station, call me.
ばConditional form (えば/ければ)If — general/hypothetical conditions. Focuses on the condition itself.安ければやすければ、買う — If it's cheap, I'll buy it.
ならPlain form / noun / な-adj stemIf (it's the case that) — reacting to something the listener said or a known situation.日本に行くにほんにいくなら、京都がいいきょうとがいい— If you're going to Japan, Kyoto is good.
とPlain present formWhen/if — automatic, habitual, or natural results. Cannot be used with volition/requests.春になるとはるになると、桜が咲くさくらがさく— When spring comes, the cherry blossoms bloom.
とき（に）/ 時（に）Plain form + とき (な-adj + な + とき, noun + の + とき)When — a time/occasion when something happens. Dictionary form = action not yet done; た form = action already done.日本に行ったにほんにいったとき、お寺を見たおてらをみた— When I went to Japan, I saw temples.
日本に行くとき、パスポートがいる — When (before) going to Japan, you need a passport.

Sequence / And / Then

JoinerAttaches toNuanceExample
て formVerb て formAnd then — sequential actions or simultaneous states.起きておきて、顔を洗ってかおをあらって、朝ごはんを食べた — I woke up, washed my face, and ate breakfast.
てからて form + からAfter doing — emphasizes that the first action is completed before the second starts.宿題をしてからしゅくだいをしてから、遊ぶあそぶ— After doing homework, I'll play.
たあとで / あとでた form + あとでAfter — the second action happens some time after the first.食べたあとで、散歩したさんぽした— After eating, I took a walk.
前にまえに辞書形 + 前にBefore doing something.寝る前にねるまえに、本を読むほんをよむ— Before sleeping, I read a book.
ながらます stem + ながらWhile doing — two simultaneous actions. The main action is the second clause.音楽を聞きながらおんがくをききながら、勉強するべんきょうする— I study while listening to music.
たり～たりするた form + りDoing things like ... and ... — non-exhaustive list of actions.週末は映画を見たりえいがをみたり、買い物したりかいものしたりする — On weekends I watch movies, go shopping, etc.

Purpose / In order to

JoinerAttaches toNuanceExample
ために辞書形 / noun + の + ためにIn order to — purpose (intentional). Also "because of" with た form.日本語を勉強するためににほんごをべんきょうするために、日本に来た — I came to Japan to study Japanese.
ように辞書形 / ない form + ようにSo that — goal/hope, often for things not fully in your control.忘れないわすれないように、メモした — I took notes so I wouldn't forget.
のに辞書形 / noun + にFor the purpose of — what something is used for.この道具は料理を作るりょうりをつくるのに使うつかう— This tool is used for cooking.

Addition / Moreover

JoinerAttaches toNuanceExample
しPlain formAnd also / besides — listing multiple reasons or qualities.頭もいいあたまもいいし、優しいやさしいし — They're smart, and kind too.
それにSentence-initialMoreover / on top of that — adds supporting information.安いし、それに、おいしい — It's cheap, and on top of that, it's delicious.
その上そのうえSentence-initialFurthermore — more formal than それに.経験があるけいけんがある。その上、資格も持っているしかくももっている— They have experience. Furthermore, they have qualifications.
上にうえにPlain form + 上に (な-adj + な + 上に)In addition to — adds to what was already said, often compounding.雨が降った上にふったうえに、風も強かったかぜもつよかった— On top of the rain, the wind was strong too.
