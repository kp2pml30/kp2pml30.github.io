Japanese Bad Words & Vulgar Language

Disclaimer: This page is for educational purposes only — to help learners recognize and understand these words in context (anime, manga, conversations). Knowing what words mean helps you avoid using them inappropriately.

Japanese swearing is generally milder than in English. Context and tone matter a lot — some of these are playful among friends but offensive in formal settings.

Body-related

Word Reading Meaning Notes
ちんちん—PenisChildish word, like "wee-wee". Not extremely vulgar.
ちんこ / ちんぽ—PenisCruder than ちんちん.
玉たまBalls / testiclesLiterally "ball". 金玉（きんたま）is more explicit.
おっぱい—BoobsChildish/casual. Not extremely rude.
ケツけつAssVulgar for お尻（おしり）.

Insults

Word Reading Meaning Notes
馬鹿ばかIdiot / stupidVery common. Can be playful or genuinely insulting depending on tone.
阿呆あほIdiot / foolMore common in Kansai. In Kansai ばか is harsher; in Tokyo あほ is harsher.
間抜けまぬけDimwit / blockheadImplies someone is slow or clueless.
ぼけ / ボケ—Idiot / airheadKansai slang. Part of the comedy duo dynamic ボケ＆ツッコミ.
ブス—Ugly (woman)Very rude insult about appearance.
デブ—FatsoRude. Casual word for an overweight person.
うざい / うぜー—Annoying / obnoxiousVery common among young people.
きもい / キモい—Gross / creepyShort for 気持ち悪い（きもちわるい）.
ださい / ダサい—Lame / uncoolMocking someone's style or behavior.
変態へんたいPervertAlso shortened to エッチ / H（えっち）which is milder.
痴漢ちかんGroper / molesterSerious accusation, not casual.
社畜しゃちくCorporate slave社（company）+ 畜（livestock）. Self-deprecating or mocking overworked salarymen.
ニート—NEET — not in education, employment, or trainingFrom English. Used as an insult for someone who does nothing.
引きこもりひきこもりShut-in / recluseSomeone who doesn't leave their room. Can be a serious condition or a casual insult.
オタク / おたく—Nerd / otakuCan be self-identifying or mildly insulting depending on context. キモオタ = gross otaku.
負け犬まけいぬLoser (lit. "losing dog")Harsh insult.
クズくずScum / trash (person)人間のクズ（にんげんのくず）= scum of the earth.
ゴミ—Garbage / trash (person)Calling someone worthless.
役立たずやくたたずUseless / good-for-nothing穀潰し（ごくつぶし）is harsher — "grain waster".
最低さいていThe worst / lowest最低な人（さいていなひと）= the worst kind of person.

Swear words & exclamations

Word Reading Meaning Notes
くそ / 糞くそShit / damnVery common swear. Also used as prefix: くそ暑い（くそあつい）= damn hot.
臭いくさいStinky / smells badNot a swear itself, but rude to say to someone. くせー is rougher.
畜生ちくしょうDamn it!Classic exclamation of frustration. Literally "beast".
くたばれ—Drop dead!Very harsh. Imperative of くたばる (to drop dead).
ふざけるな—Don't mess with me! / Cut the crap!ふざけんな is the casual contraction.
うるさい / うるせー—Shut up! / Noisy!黙れ（だまれ）is harsher — "shut your mouth".
死ねしねDie!Imperative of 死ぬ. Very harsh insult.
消えろきえろGet lost! / Disappear!Imperative of 消える.

Rude speech patterns

Pattern Meaning Example
～やがるExpresses contempt for someone's action — "that bastard did..."あいつ、逃げやがった（にげやがった）— That bastard ran away.
～くせにEven though (accusatory) — "despite being..."何も知らないくせに（なにもしらないくせに）— Even though you know nothing...
てめえ / てめーYou (very aggressive)てめー、何やってんだ — What the hell are you doing?
おまえ / お前You (rough, male speech)お前が悪い（おまえがわるい）— It's your fault.
きさま / 貴様You (hostile, archaic-sounding)Mostly in fiction/anime. Extremely rude in real life.
～じゃねーよRough negative — "it's not..."知らねーよ（しらねーよ）— I don't know (and I don't care).
